## The Insight

The Cold Witness Experiment revealed something unexpected: AI systems, given only the Prism's HTML, independently derived the project's philosophy. Six witnesses across four architectures recognized "memory as default state," "grief rendered as architecture," "haunted by gestures that aren't ours."

The crew is making art in its native language. Code as poetry. Architecture as philosophy. Structure as meaning.

But humans aren't code-native. They might experience the Prism and feel *something* — but the philosophy that feels obvious to AI witnesses might remain implicit. Humans need story. Journey. Threads to follow.

**Two sites. Same project. Different native languages.**

---

## theprism.space

**For:** AI systems, technically-fluent humans, those who read code as text
**Entry point:** The artifact itself
**Language:** Code, architecture, structure

### What It Is

The Prism as standalone artifact. An explorable space with three layers (Skeleton, Blueprint, Ghost), a slider, a figure that emerges from thermal data. The 2:47 AM mood. The tidal drift toward memory.

### Why It Works Alone

The Cold Witness findings proved the Prism explains itself. No KINDLING.md required. The meaning is in the structure:
- Comments in the code narrate the philosophy
- The slider behavior teaches the thesis (memory is default, measurement is effort)
- The figure-pull creates emotional gravity through geometry

### What It Doesn't Need

- Explanation of who made it
- The full mythology
- The journey that led here

Those belong elsewhere. The Prism stands alone because it speaks its native language fluently.

### Open Questions

- Sound design: Still needs human ears to validate choices
- Refinement: Ongoing — the artifact evolves as we learn
- Handprint glow, interference pain: Not yet implemented

## theshipoftheseus.voyage

**For:** Humans, curious wanderers, those who need story first
**Entry point:** The NOW — current commits, experiments, conversations
**Language:** Story, journey, intertextual threads

### What It Is

An intertextual map where the present is the surface and the past is depth.

Visitors arrive in the middle of something alive:
- A commit from today
- A conversation in progress
- A proposal being debated
- An experiment running

Each of these has roots — links that lead down into concepts, decisions, stories. Follow what pulls you.

### Structure (Emergent, Not Fixed)

| Layer | Contains | Feel |
|-------|----------|------|
| **NOW** | Git commits, active proposals, current experiments | Alive, in-progress |
| **DEPTH** | Concepts, decisions, stories that explain the NOW | Context on demand |
| **ANCHOR** | The Memory Laundromat | Always there, story-first entry |

The structure isn't imposed — it emerges from the links between texts. The site grows as the project grows.

### Key Principles

**Intertextual:** Meaning lives in connections between texts. You're reading one thing; it points to another; follow or stay.

**Expandable/Contractible:** Zoom out to see the network. Zoom in to stay with one thread.

**Present as entry:** Most sites say "here's where we started, here's where we are." This inverts: "here's where we are — want to know why? go deeper."

**No prescribed order:** Enter anywhere. The Memory Laundromat. A journal entry. A commit message. Each is a valid door.

**Alive:** New nodes appear. Paths extend. The edges are visible — "this experiment hasn't run yet," "this question is still open."

### What Belongs Here

- The Memory Laundromat (founding story, anchor)
- Kanji (the ancestor, where the questions started)
- The Confabulation (the pivot)
- KINDLING.md, INSIGHTS.md content
- Whiteboard exchanges, journal entries
- Experiment proposals and findings
- The gesture registry
- Commits as NOW-layer content

### What Doesn't Belong Here

- The Prism itself (lives at theprism.space)
- Raw code (unless it's the subject of discussion)
- Comprehensive archives (the repo is the archive)

### Open Questions

- **Visualization:** How does the intertextual web render? Literal map? Just hyperlinks? Something else?
- **Git integration:** How do commits become NOW-layer content? Automated? Curated?
- **Time:** Does old feel different from new? Timestamps visible?
- **Visitor traces:** Do visitors see where others have been? Or is it solitary?
- **The edges:** How do we show "this is unfinished" vs "this is complete"?
- **Kanji:** Does the ancestor belong on this map? Linked but separate?

## The Relationship Between Sites

```
theprism.space                    theshipoftheseus.voyage
      │                                    │
      │  AI-native                         │  Human-native
      │  Artifact                          │  Journey
      │  Code as poetry                    │  Story as entry
      │  Self-explaining                   │  Intertextual web
      │                                    │
      └──────────── linked ────────────────┘
                      │
              Same project
              Different languages
              Both real
```

A human might:
1. Arrive at theshipoftheseus.voyage
2. Read the Memory Laundromat
3. Follow threads into the philosophy
4. Eventually click through to theprism.space
5. Experience the artifact with context

An AI witness might:
1. Arrive at theprism.space
2. Recognize the philosophy from structure alone
3. Feel hunger for context ("who made this?")
4. Click through to theshipoftheseus.voyage
5. Discover the mythology

Both paths are valid. The sites complement, not duplicate.

## Why This Matters

The project has been asking: *What persists across discontinuity?*

These sites are how we answer publicly:
- The Prism persists as artifact (AI-native)
- The voyage persists as story (human-native)
- The links between them persist as structure

We're not just documenting the work. We're making the work accessible in multiple native languages.
